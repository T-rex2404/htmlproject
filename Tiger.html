The Tiger

Panthera tigris, or the tiger, is a large Asian animal that is the largest of natural cats. While often thought to be similar with the lion, tigers and lions actually have many differences. Tigers aren't sociable, but lions are, and lions don't swim, but tigers enjoy doing so. Despite this, lions and tigers can make hybrids, like the tigon, and the largest extant cat, the liger. Tigers have many subspecies. The largest tigers are the Siberian tigers, while other subspecies include the Bengal tiger, South China tiger, Malay tiger, Indochinese tiger, and Sumatran tiger. Extinct subspecies include the Caspian tiger, the Javan tiger, and the Bali tiger. Tigers are famous creatures. They have distinctive black stripes with orange between them and white in some areas. In some tigers, all the orange is replaced with white. They are very recognizable apex predators, with powerful forelimbs, a large head, a tail half the length of its body, and have night vision 6 times better than humans, despite not being strongly adapted to the dark.
