Description

The elephant is a magnificent animal. It is the largest land animal. It consists of the Asian elephant, the African forest elephant, and the largest, the African bush elephant. The Asian elephant has 3 subspecies, one which resides in the Asian mainland, another in Borneo, and another in Sumatra. African elephants have large ears that help disperse heat so the animal doesn't overheat under the hot African sun. The animal's trunk is a part of the area between the upper lip and nose that enlarged over millions of years due to mutations. It uses the trunk the same way as our fingers, and the tip of the trunk is about as sensitive as our fingertips. The Asian elephant is smaller and has smaller ears. Both elephants are brown in color. Elephants are the only survivors among a group of mammals called proboscideans, which also include the extinct mammoths and mastodons. Elephants are known for being very intelligent and having great memories, but humans treat them rather badly. Even then, you can still ride an elephant, for the most, it's a gentle, caring, and family creature that longs to have a mate. It sends sounds with frequencies too low for our ears through the ground, which are picked up by other elephants by their delicate soles. Mostly, a group of elephants is led by the oldest and wisest female who knows the location of sources of food and water. But still, elephants can attack, and kill 500 people per year.
